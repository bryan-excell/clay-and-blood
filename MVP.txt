# Clay and Blood: Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

## Purpose

The MVP should prove the core loop: explore an interconnected stage world, fight real-time server-authoritative encounters, collect equipment and resources, return to safety, and push farther out again.

The project is past the earliest foundation stage. Health/stamina/mana, inventory/loadout, vendors/upgrades, combat authority, vision, possession/corpses, and reliable exits all have working implementations. The current MVP focus is the world structure and content pass.

## Current Development Frame

Treat MVP work as three foundations rather than a pile of unrelated features:

1. **Combat resources/state**
   - Shared resource schema for health, stamina, and mana.
   - Server-authoritative spend, regen, damage, death, and recovery behavior.
   - Combat state hooks for sprinting, dashing, casting, attacks, flinch, stagger, and poise.
   - Status: mostly implemented and usable.

2. **Player/world data model**
   - Inventory, equipment, loadouts, currency, pickups, drops, buy/sell, and upgrades.
   - Stable stage IDs, stage metadata, zone metadata, exit identity, spawn rules, and world entity definitions.
   - Status: inventory/progression data is mostly implemented; world identity exists but needs a more complete MVP pass.

3. **Content built on those systems**
   - Authored town/hub spaces.
   - Procedural or semi-procedural wilderness/exploration/dungeon stages.
   - Spawn tables, difficulty bands, loot placement, hidden shortcut, and enough encounter variety for a short play session.
   - Status: active focus.

## MVP Scope

### Stage-Based World

- **Minimum Connected World**
  - Town hub with inn/shop/safe respawn.
  - Gate or road transition out of town.
  - Wilderness area with enemies and resources.
  - Exploration zone with larger layout and one hidden or optional connection.
  - Dungeon or challenge pocket with denser combat.

- **Connections**
  - Exits must be stable, named, and deterministic.
  - Core travel paths should be bidirectional unless intentionally marked otherwise.
  - Arrival placement must be safe and should avoid immediate re-trigger loops.
  - At least one shortcut or hidden connection should reward exploration.

- **Generation**
  - Keep authored stages for important places like town, inn, shop, and key transition points.
  - Use deterministic procedural generation for wilds/exploration/dungeon areas.
  - Add generator variants or config profiles for wilderness, cave/dungeon, and road/transition stages.
  - Generation must expose enough metadata for spawns, exits, terrain, and future persistence.

### Combat

- Player movement, sprint, dash, melee, ranged/spell options, and resource costs.
- Health/stamina/mana with shared authoritative rules.
- Hit reactions, poise, flinch/stagger, and death/respawn.
- Enemy behavior sufficient for MVP:
  - Aggressive enemy that hunts and attacks.
  - Neutral or guard-like entity with faction/team behavior.
  - Passive/fleeing enemy is desirable but can be cut if time is tight.

### Progression and Economy

- Inventory and loadout.
- Pickups, drops, and currency.
- Simple merchant buy/sell.
- Basic weapon/spell upgrade path.
- Equipment should visibly affect combat or survivability.
- Full skill trees, stat allocation, and complex crafting are out of scope.

### Save/Load

Save/load comes after resource, inventory, and stage identity schemas are stable enough to avoid churn.

For MVP, persist only:

- Player identity.
- Current/last safe position.
- Resources and death-relevant state.
- Inventory, loadout, currency, and upgrade levels.
- Minimal world progression flags such as discovered shortcut or defeated challenge state.

A full account system is not required for MVP unless multiplayer identity becomes a near-term requirement.

### Minimal Leveling

Leveling is intentionally late and small:

- XP and level count.
- A few unlocks or upgrades.
- No full skill tree for MVP.

## Out of Scope

- Full skill trees or deep stat allocation.
- Complex crafting economy.
- Quest system.
- Campsite system.
- Companion system beyond possession/corpse mechanics already present.
- Teleportation network.
- Large world expansion beyond the MVP route.
- Full account/login system unless needed for deployment.

## Current Milestones

1. **Completed/Foundation: Combat Resources**
   - Health, stamina, and mana schema.
   - Regen/spend/damage rules.
   - Server authority and HUD-facing summaries.

2. **Completed/Foundation: Inventory and Equipment**
   - Owned items, currency, pickups, drops.
   - Loadouts and equipment switching.
   - Merchant buy/sell and basic upgrades.

3. **Active: World Structure and Generation**
   - Define the MVP world graph.
   - Add stable stage/zone/difficulty metadata.
   - Create generator profiles for wilds, exploration, and dungeon spaces.
   - Move spawn placement toward stage-aware spawn tables.
   - Add topology and generation tests.

4. **Next: Save/Load**
   - Serialize the now-stable player and world-facing data.
   - Keep the first persistence format minimal.

5. **Later: Minimal Progression**
   - XP, levels, and a few unlocks.
   - Avoid full skill-tree work until the core loop is fun.

6. **Final: Polish and Playtest**
   - Balance enemy placement and rewards.
   - Improve feedback, UI clarity, and performance.
   - Make the first 15-30 minutes coherent.

## Evaluation Criteria

The MVP succeeds when it demonstrates:

1. Interconnected exploration across named stages with reliable exits.
2. Deterministic generation that supports repeatable navigation and content placement.
3. Real-time combat that feels readable and resource-driven.
4. A simple but motivating loot/vendor/upgrade loop.
5. Stable death/respawn and short-session progression.
6. Enough content for 15-30 minutes of play without needing future systems to make sense.
